Blue Whale

Profile

The Blue Whale is a marine mammal, that’s believed to be the
largest animal that has ever lived, far bigger than even the
largest dinosaur. They habitat the Antarctic Ocean, the North
Atlantic, North & South Pacific and Indian Oceans.
They are members of the order Artiodactyla and family Balaenopteridae
(or ‘Rorquals’), the largest group of Baleen whales, which includes the Humpback Whale.

Details

Scientific name: Balaenoptera Musculus

Length: 98 - 110feet

Lifespan: 80 - 90years

Weight: 300,000 - 400,000pounds

Diet: Krill

Habitat: Ocean

Top Speed: 50kph (31Mph)

Color: Deep Blue-Grey

No. Of Species: 1

Conservation status: Endangered

Sound: Clicks, Whistle and Pulsed Calls

Fact

The blue whale have a carnivore diet, but despite of their size, these
massive sea creatures prefer to eat some of the tiniest marine life.
Blue Whales feast on plankton called krill, which is a
small animal that resembles shrimp. Due to the relative sizes of both
the blue whale and krill, they can consume as many as 40 million krill,
or 8,000 pounds (3,600 kg) of it daily.
